University of California, Riverside, CA

MS in Computer Engineering, March 2017
Project: Area Optimization of VLSI Power Network with Tree-based EM Constraints
Chair, (Project Advisor): Dr. Sheldon Tan
Committee Member: Dr. Daniel Wong

As power supply voltage continues to drop with the VLSI technology scaling, power network design becomes a very challenging task. It is important to minimize chip area for network routing while avoiding potential reliability failures.

Electromigration (EM) is the movement of metal ions as the result of the flow of electrical charges through the metal wires in the chip. Traditional EM analysis is based on current density, defined simply as the current per unit width. However, current density-based assessment does not work well for multi-branch interconnect wires as the stresses developed in each wire segment is not independent of one another

This project presents an EM immortality constraint for general multi-branch interconnect trees. This voltage-based EM assessment technique can naturally comprehend the impact of the topology of the wire structure on EM-induced stress.
